Ann McFerran

The founder of Glamnetic, Ann McFerran came a long way from immigrating to the U.S. from Thailand at age 7. Having graduated from UCLA with a Bachelor of Science and founded her own company Glamnetic, McFerran is one of the people on Forbes’ “30 Under 30 - Retail and E-commerce” for 2021. Glamnetic, which McFerran is the founder and CEO of, is an E-commerce company based in Los Angeles that specializes in magnetic eyelashes and eyeliners. The company found immediate success, earning $50 million in sales in its first year. Ann McFerran continues to make a name for herself in the cosmetic industry and Glamentic continues to be sold in multiple stores, both in person and online.

Babba Rivera

Originally from Stockholm, Sweden, Babba Rivera is the founder and CEO of Ceremonia, a Latinx hair care brand that vows to provide its customers with rich formulas that will promote healthy hair. Not only is she the owner of Ceremonia, but Rivera is also an award-winning businesswoman, being included in Forbes’ 2016 “30 Under 30 - Marketing and Advertising” list for her work as a marketing manager at Uber. Additionally, Rivera was acknowledged as “Sweden’s Top Talent” of 2016 by Veckans Affärer, one of the biggest business magazines in the country. To this day, Babba Rivera pursues innovation — in the first half of 2023, she raised $10 million in a Series A funding round that has allowed Ceremonia products to be sold in over 500 Sephora stores.

Madam C.J. Walker

From growing up in poverty in the Southern United States to becoming one of the most successful businesswomen in the country, Madam C.J. Walker crashed all stereotypes. After dealing with financial stress, physical stress, and hair loss, Walker started her career working for African American businesswoman Annie Turbo Malone as a sales agent. After getting married, she then decided to start her own business selling hair care products and straighteners for African American women with just $1.25. After years of hard work, some creative juices, and an entrepreneurial spirit, she was able to make it big with sales exceeding $500,000 in the final year of her life.
